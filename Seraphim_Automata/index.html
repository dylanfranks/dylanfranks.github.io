Description

Seraphim Automata is a 2d game that allows you to generate music in a variety of styles with cellular automata.

There are 80 levels total. The player progresses by avoiding the cells while shooting them. Rooms and time signatures get increasingly smaller the higher they progress. Tempo-changing powerups allow the player to alter the music in real-time.

This was Dylan's first project to explore procedural music generation. It was originally designed specifically to replicate the improvisational nature of jazz music. This game can trace its roots back to the generative music heralded by Brian Eno.

Features

  • Procedurally generated music
  • 13 genres of music (classical, jazz, choral, gamelan, etc)
  • 12 scales (major, minor, whole tone, chromatic, etc)
  • 12 musical keys
  • 8 time signatures that each create different patterns
  • Keyboard displays active notes
  • Made in 5 weeks
